Discover Kigali

Kigali, the capital city of Rwanda, is a dynamic and clean city known for its hospitality, progressive development, and scenic hills. It serves as the economic, political, and cultural center of Rwanda.

Brief History

Founded in 1907 during the German colonial era, Kigali became the capital in 1962 at Rwanda’s independence. Since then, it has grown into a model African city known for urban planning, peace, and innovation.

Current Demographics

  • Population: ~1.2 million (2024)
  • Languages: Kinyarwanda, English, French
  • Main Industries: Tourism, ICT, Finance, Real Estate
